Resume

Coloratura soprano HELEN DONALDSON has performed extensively in her native Australia and is also a citizen of the United States.

Fall 2002, Helen sang the title role in the world premiere of Kyle Gann’s Cinderella’s Bad Magic in Russia at Moscow’s Alternativa Festival which was later reprised in St. Peterburg. In Fall 2003 she won second place and the Popular Jury Award at the Bidu Sayao International Vocal Competition in Brazil. In 2004 Helen played Norina in Don Pasquale for Opera Queensland and in 2005 performed Nannetta in Verdi’s Falstaff for Opera Theater of St. Louis. Helen appeared as Jenny in Off-Center’s west coast premiere production of the contemporary opera Black Water at the Seattle Center House Theatre in 2006.

She has performed the role of Giunia in Lucio Silla for the Lyric Opera of Queensland as well as Titania in A Midsummer Night’s Dream and Musetta in La Boheme and Miss Wordsworth in Albert Herring in Hong Kong. Miss Donaldson has also sung Josephine in HMS Pinafore, Yum-Yum in The Mikado and Mabel in The Pirates of Penzance, all of which toured throughout Australia and New Zealand and were subsequently recorded for CD and video. In 1999 she sang both Anna Glawari and Valencienne in The Merry Widow in Brisbane, Adelaide, Melbourne and Sydney. This was followed by a tour of A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum playing Philia.

Helen has performed concerts and recitals throughout Australia and Hong Kong while attaining her graduate music degrees at the Queensland Conservatorium of Music. In the United States she was a semi-finalist for the 2002 Licia Albanese Puccini Foundation Award and currently resides in the New York City area.
